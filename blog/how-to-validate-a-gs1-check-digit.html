How To Validate a GS1 Check Digit

A simple explanation of the GS1 Mod 10 approach

Home > Blog > How To Validate a GS1 Check Digit

Why the Check Digit Matters

The check digit helps verify that a GS1 identifier has been constructed correctly and reduces the chance of common data-entry mistakes.

How the Calculation Works

Starting from the right, digits are weighted by alternating multipliers. Their sum is used to determine the final digit needed to make the total align with the GS1 Mod 10 rule.

Where You See It

GS1 check digits appear in identifiers such as GTIN, GLN, SSCC, and other key numbering systems used in trade and logistics.

Practical Tip

If an identifier fails validation, first check for transposed digits, missing leading zeros, or an incorrect base length before assuming the entire number is invalid.
