What Is the Difference Between GLN and SGLN?

Choosing the right GS1 location identifier for the job

Home > Blog > What Is the Difference Between GLN and SGLN?

GLN at a Glance

A GLN, or Global Location Number, identifies a legal entity, functional entity, or physical location using a 13-digit GS1 number structure.

SGLN at a Glance

An SGLN extends that location identity by adding an extension or serial component. That makes it more specific when a plain GLN is not enough.

When To Use GLN

Use a GLN when you need a standard location identifier for a business, warehouse, store, department, or other recognized location reference.

When To Use SGLN

Use an SGLN when you need to identify a more precise serialized or extended location context, especially in EPC and traceability-related workflows.
